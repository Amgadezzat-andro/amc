Lebanon Tax Reform 2025: What Businesses Need to Know

By AMC Tax Advisory Team
March 15, 2026
6 min read
1,240 views

The Lebanese government has introduced significant amendments to corporate and income tax regulations for the 2025–2026 fiscal period. These changes affect filing deadlines, VAT calculations, and cross-border transactions — with compliance timelines already in effect.

Overview of the Tax Amendments

Lebanon's Ministry of Finance issued Circular No. 2025/12 in November 2025, outlining a series of structural reforms to the tax code. The primary objective is to broaden the fiscal base, improve tax compliance through digitisation, and align Lebanese tax standards with regional best practices.

These amendments represent the most substantial revision to Lebanon's tax framework since the 2002 reforms, and businesses of all sizes — from startups to large corporations — are directly impacted. Understanding the changes is essential for maintaining compliance and avoiding penalties.
